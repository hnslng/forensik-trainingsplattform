<h2 class="section-title"><span class="number">7.3</span> DNS-Record-Typen</h2><p>DNS speichert verschiedene Arten von Informationen &ndash; sogenannte <strong>Records</strong>:</p><div class="table-container"><table><thead><tr><th>Record</th><th>Funktion</th><th>Beispiel</th><th>Erkl&auml;rung</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>A</strong></td><td>IPv4-Adresse</td><td><span class="inline-code">example.com &rarr; 93.184.216.34</span></td><td>Die wichtigste Zuordnung</td></tr><tr><td><strong>AAAA</strong></td><td>IPv6-Adresse</td><td><span class="inline-code">example.com &rarr; 2606:2800:220:...</span></td><td>Wie A, aber f&uuml;r IPv6</td></tr><tr><td><strong>CNAME</strong></td><td>Alias</td><td><span class="inline-code">www &rarr; example.com</span></td><td>Verweis auf einen anderen Namen</td></tr><tr><td><strong>MX</strong></td><td>Mail-Server</td><td><span class="inline-code">example.com &rarr; mail.example.com</span></td><td>Wohin E-Mails geschickt werden</td></tr><tr><td><strong>NS</strong></td><td>Nameserver</td><td><span class="inline-code">example.com &rarr; ns1.example.com</span></td><td>Wer f&uuml;r diese Domain zust&auml;ndig ist</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TXT</strong></td><td>Freier Text</td><td>SPF, DKIM</td><td>F&uuml;r E-Mail-Sicherheit &amp; Verifikation</td></tr><tr><td><strong>PTR</strong></td><td>Reverse DNS</td><td><span class="inline-code">93.184.216.34 &rarr; example.com</span></td><td>IP &rarr; Name (umgekehrt)</td></tr></tbody></table></div><blockquote><p><strong>Tipp:</strong> Der Record-Typ sagt dir, <em>welche Art</em> von Information du abfragst. <span class="inline-code">A</span> = IP-Adresse, <span class="inline-code">MX</span> = Mail-Server, <span class="inline-code">NS</span> = Nameserver.</p></blockquote>
